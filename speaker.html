Keynote Speakers

Prof. Dr. Noor Zaman Jhanjhi
Dr. N.Z. Jhanjhi is a renowned Computer Science professor specializing in Cybersecurity at Taylor's University, Malaysia. He holds leadership roles including Program Director for Postgraduate Research, Acting Program Director for Master of Applied Computing, and Director of the Center for Smart Society.
Ranked in the world's top 2% of research scientists, he's Malaysia's leading computer science researcher according to Scopus. Recognized as an Outstanding faculty member by MDEC Malaysia (2022), his research has an Impact Factor exceeding 900 and impressive citation metrics. With international patents and authorship of 40+ research books, he's supervised numerous scholars, served editorial roles, and received accolades like Outstanding Associate Editor at IEEE ACCESS. Dr. Jhanjhi's expertise spans Cybersecurity, IoT, Data Science, Software Engineering, and UAVs, and he's well-versed in academic qualifications such as ABET, NCAAA, and NCEAC.

Dr. Hong Lin
Dr. Hong Lin, a respected figure in the realm of computer science, brings a wealth of expertise to the field. Holding a Ph.D. in Computer Science from the University of Science and Technology of China, his academic journey has been marked by groundbreaking work. With a background in parallel programming models and real-world implementations, Dr. Lin's contributions span domains such as computational biology at Purdue University and network security at Nokia, Inc. Since 2001, he has been a driving force at the University of Houston-Downtown (UHD), establishing the Grid Computing Lab through an NSF MRI grant.

His mentorship and commitment extend beyond academia, with roles as a Scholars Academy mentor, REU faculty mentor, and CAHSI faculty mentor. Currently, he's delving into brain state analysis, exploring the effects of meditative practices on cognition. In essence, Dr. Hong Lin's journey encapsulates a commitment to innovation and collaboration that continues to enrich the world of computer science.
